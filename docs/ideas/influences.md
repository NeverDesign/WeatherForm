# Weatherform — Influences

> **Purpose:** Gameplay and artistic references that inform design decisions. Consult this when evaluating mechanics, visual direction, or tone.

---

## Gameplay Influences

| Title | What It Contributes |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Advance Wars** | The structural ancestor. Grid-based army tactics, unit types with distinct movement and attack ranges, capturing objectives to sustain the war effort. The feel of committing a force to a map and outmaneuvering through positioning. |
| **Wargroove** | Modern spiritual successor to Advance Wars with a fantasy skin. Confirms the genre's viability for contemporary players; commander abilities show how a signature powerful unit can add personality without breaking balance. |
| **Hearthstone** | UI clarity and game feel. Each card has a clean, readable identity at a glance — cost, attack, health, effect. The satisfaction of discrete, weighty actions. Onboarding through approachability without sacrificing depth. |
| **StarCraft** | The design philosophy reference (see overview.md). You pick a race before the match and cannot change it — your macro identity is fixed, your micro wins or loses the game. WeatherForm's element-as-army-commitment maps directly to this. |

## Artistic Influences

| Title / Artist | What It Contributes |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **Moebius** | Clean linework, bold flat color, strange-but-grounded environments. His desert and elemental landscapes are a tonal touchstone for Dune and the wider WeatherForm world. |
| **The Legend of Zelda: Wind Waker HD** | Cel-shaded rendering, expressive characters, a saturated and timeless palette. The aesthetic shows how bold outlines and flat shading can feel both cartoonish and emotionally resonant. |
| **The Legend of Zelda: Minish Cap** | Top-down sprite art with intricate small-scale world design. Directly relevant to the board perspective — the level of detail that fits a tile, how units read clearly from above. |
| **OlliOlli World** | Bold flat vector shapes, high-saturation colors, strong silhouettes, playful character personality. A reference for the modern indie aesthetic WeatherForm should aim for. |
| **Adventure Time** | Irregular organic shapes, thick outlines, pastel-meets-bright color combinations. Shows how elemental worlds (Fire Kingdom, Ice Kingdom, etc.) can feel distinct through color and shape language alone. |
